Technical Skills

Android

I've been working on Android Apps for 5 years now and I really love it. It have been 5 years of intense development, having a 8 hour job and extra projects. I always like to joke that I should add 50% to my years of experience for the extra projects that have thought me a lot.

Given the fact that Android is so wide spread I had the chance to work on different kids of Apps. The first Android code I've seen was in Eclipse and it was an eBook Reader (Polo). Now, I work with Android Studio and the cool tools that the AOSP team comes with, together with IntelliJ and the whole community. I've been in the embedded area of Android with Twinca and DentCall Touch but I've also worked on Apps that are on the Play store and downloaded by thoulsands, like Mobiloud or Skivision.

Parts of the framework and libraries that I've used are:

  • Network and Connectivity: Bluetooth and WiFi
  • Camera API
  • Realm (and the built in SQLite Database)
  • Fabric (Crashlytics)
  • Analytics, Segment, Taplytics, Intercom
  • Square Suite: OkHttp, Picasso, Retrofit

I've seen and refactored lots of code that was really wasting important memory and CPU resources and made apps smooth.

    Core principles

    • Billing time is working time - I never add hours that I don't work and once the timer has started I stop doing anything other than client work. The time billed is time offered to the client's needs but on the other hand, never expect me to spend a second without billing. 5 minutes conversations included.
    • Flexibility all the way - everyone has different needs, including me. I understand client-specific needs but I need them to understand my needs also. I think good communication and planning can make life easy for everyone.
    • I am the specialist - let's face it: if you did not need a specialist, you wouldn't have reached me; so either you let me be the specialist and take care of the technical part or let's be friends and not work together. I can understand that there are lines where business pushes technical in one direction or another but let me worry about the technical things and really consider my advice because they are based on experience.
    • Quality is not negotiable - because a lot of reasons: I'm the one dealing with the code and I don't want to have to deal with garbage. You'll blame me if it becomes a stinky pile of garbage. You might want someone else to take over at some point. It is cheaper to build quality things in the end. Final users appreciate it.
    • You are the client but I'm the end-users' advocate - Mobile Apps Users have some specific needs and expect the Apps to behave in some ways based on the platform's specific (iOS or Android). My goal is to make Apps that are very friendly to the end user because this is the only way that they will be happy and keep the apps. Usually, both technical and business people tend to ignore the end user.
    • Every story has an end - either the job has a clear time-frame or I have my own. We need to friendly agree how long a job takes until we can hand it over to someone else and how we will do this. Maybe it won't ever be necessary but better be prepared for it anyway
    • I'm not a tester - As much as I would like to be perfect, I'm not. Every product needs a quality assurance and by definition that can't be done by the same person that creates the product itself because he is blind to his own mistakes. Either you have a tester or you let me hire one for the job but never consider that I can also test my own software to perfection
